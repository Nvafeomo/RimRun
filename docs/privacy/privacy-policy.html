1. What information do we collect?

Personal information you disclose to us

In short: We collect personal information that you provide to us.

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide when you register on the Services, express an interest in obtaining information about us or our products and Services, participate in activities on the Services, or otherwise contact us.

Personal information provided by you. The personal information we collect depends on the context of your interactions with us and the Services, the choices you make, and the products and features you use. It may include:

  • Account details: Email address and username when you create or update your account.
  • Profile information: Profile photo (optional), and any other details you choose to add to your profile.
  • Date of birth: Required during onboarding for age verification purposes only. We use this to apply age-appropriate defaults (for example, certain privacy settings default to more restrictive values for users under 18).
  • Chat messages: Content you send in direct messages, group chats, and court chats. Messages are stored to deliver real-time and historical chat functionality.
  • Court contributions: Courts you submit, edit, or vote on (verify/flag), and courts you subscribe to. When you add a court you provide its public street address and map location. This court information is public and visible to other users — you should only enter the public address of a basketball court, never a home or private address. Adding a court requires you to be at least 16 years old.
  • Content reports: If you report another user or piece of content, we store the report details to allow moderation review.

Sensitive information. We collect date of birth solely for age verification. We do not process other sensitive categories of personal information (such as financial data, biometric identifiers, or health information).

Social login data. You may register using a third-party account (for example, Google). If you do, we collect certain profile information as described in How do we handle your social logins?

Application data. If you use our application(s), we may collect the following if you grant access or permission:

  • Geolocation. We may request location from your device while you use our app to show nearby basketball courts on the map and to support location-based search. You can change this in your device settings at any time.
  • Push notifications. We may request permission to send notifications about your account or app features. You can turn these off in your device settings.

This information helps us operate and secure our application(s), troubleshoot, and run internal analytics and reporting.

All personal information you provide must be true, complete, and accurate, and you must notify us of any changes.

Google API

Our use of information received from Google APIs follows the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

2. How do we process your information?

In short: We process your information to provide, improve, and administer our Services, communicate with you, for security and fraud prevention, and to comply with law.

We may process your personal information to:

  • Facilitate account creation and authentication and manage user accounts.
  • Deliver services you request, including real-time chat, court discovery, and map features.
  • Apply age-appropriate defaults using date of birth during onboarding (for example, restricting discoverability for users under 18).
  • Respond to inquiries and offer support.
  • Enable user-to-user communications — direct messages, group chats, and court community chats.
  • Moderate content and enforce community standards — reviewing reports, applying suspensions, and protecting users from abuse.
  • Request feedback about our Services.
  • Evaluate and improve our Services, products, marketing, and your experience.
  • Comply with legal obligations, respond to legal requests, and protect our rights.

3. Your in-app privacy controls

In short: You have direct control over key privacy settings inside the app. These take effect immediately when you change them.

You can access these settings in the app under Profile → Privacy. Each toggle saves instantly.

Public profile

👥
Show friends count
When off, other users cannot see how many friends you have on your profile.
🏀
Show courts joined
When off, other users cannot see how many courts you follow.
📍
Show courts added
When off, other users cannot see how many courts you have submitted to the app.

Messaging & discovery

💬
New DMs from friends only
When on, only people you are already friends with can start a new direct message conversation with you. Existing chats are not affected.
🔍
Show up in Add friends search
Controls whether other users can find you by username using the Add friends search (Chats → Friends → Add). Users under 18 have this off by default. Turning it off means people can only connect with you through in-app interactions such as court chats.

4. When and with whom do we share your personal information?

In short: We do not sell your personal information. We may share information in specific situations described in this section and/or with certain third parties.

We may need to share your personal information in situations such as:

  • Service providers. We use Supabase as our hosted backend, authentication, and database provider. Supabase processes data on our behalf according to their privacy policy.
  • Other users. Information you share publicly (for example, your public profile, or court information you submit) is visible to other users of the app. Messages you send in group or court chats are visible to other participants of that conversation. Court chat visibility is filtered by age: users generally only see messages from others within an age-appropriate range, so users under 18 and adults (18+) do not normally see each other's court-chat messages. Your email address and date of birth are not shared with other users.
  • Business transfers. We may share or transfer your information in connection with a merger, sale of assets, financing, or acquisition.
  • Legal requirements. We may disclose information if required by law, legal process, or to protect the rights, safety, and security of our users, the app, or others.

We retain personal information only as long as needed for the purposes in this notice, unless a longer period is required or permitted by law. No purpose in this notice requires keeping your personal information longer than while you have an account with us.

7. How do we keep your information safe?

In short: We use organizational and technical measures to protect your personal information.

We implement reasonable technical and organizational safeguards, including row-level security policies on our database to restrict data access by role, column-level restrictions so that sensitive fields such as your email address and date of birth are not readable by other users, and role-based access controls for administrative data. No electronic transmission or storage is completely secure; we cannot guarantee that unauthorized parties will never defeat our security. Use the Services at your own risk and in a secure environment.

8. Children's privacy

In short: The app is not directed at children under 13. Users under 18 receive more restrictive privacy defaults.

The App is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have collected such information, please contact us using the email below and we will take appropriate steps to remove it.

Users who are 13–17 years old may use the App with a parent or guardian's consent. We apply more restrictive defaults and safety features for users under 18, including:

  • Username discoverability in friend search is off by default.
  • Court chat messages are filtered by age, so younger users and adults do not normally see each other's messages.
  • A higher age requirement (16+) to add a court, since adding a court involves submitting a public address.

Users under 18 can review and adjust the available defaults in Profile → Privacy.

10. Controls for Do-Not-Track features

Some browsers and devices offer a Do-Not-Track (DNT) setting. There is no uniform standard for DNT yet, so we do not currently respond to DNT signals. If standards change, we will update this notice.

California law requires us to state that we do not respond to DNT signals at this time because no industry or legal standard for honoring them exists yet.
